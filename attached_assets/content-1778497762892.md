1. Jr. Monty Furya - SP → KingofJuco Viral Video, 159 km/h with sharp hard slider & power curve, intimidated College and Pros in the cages

Monty Furya, a 3-star starting pitcher, unexpectedly skyrockets to fame when he features in one of KingOf Juco's YouTube videos. In the video, Monty is seen throwing a blistering fastball at 159 km/h, complemented by a sharp, hard slider and a devastating power curve. His intimidating presence and extraordinary pitching prowess in the cages leave both college and professional players awestruck.

2. Sr. Murphy Prez - SP / RP → Presidente → Endorsement from Pro Catcher after catching the special pitch that he calls Don Trump

Murphy Preston, a 3-star JUCO senior and a staunch Republican from Iowa, finds himself in the limelight after an endorsement from pro pitchers Mitch Keller and AJ Puk. This endorsement comes following a joint bullpen session at a facility in Cedar Rapids, where Murphy showcases a unique pitch he humorously calls "The Don Trump." This special pitch, characterized by its unpredictable movement and effectiveness, impresses Keller and Puk, who speak highly of Murphy's talent and potential.

3. Fr. Leland Tyrman - RP → After getting shelled with a 0-8 record to start the HS season, coaches moved him to SS and he is now leading the league in average and stolen bases. He still has the bullseye arm strength

Leland Tyrman, initially the last-ranked prospect in his class as a 2-star relief pitcher, experiences a dramatic turnaround in his baseball career. After a disheartening 0-8 record and a 7.00 ERA in high school, his coaches make a bold decision to move him to shortstop. This change proves to be a stroke of genius as Leland quickly adapts to his new position, astonishing everyone with his natural aptitude for batting and base running. He soon leads the league in batting average and stolen bases, showcasing a newfound confidence and skill set. Coaches and scouts take note of Leland's "bullseye" throw across the diamond, a skill that echoes his pitching background.

4. Fr. Sammy Bono - SP / RP / CL → Mileman, Found to be masquerading as another player, changing his name

Devin Bennett, initially celebrated as a versatile 3-star freshman pitcher excelling in starting, relieving, and closing roles, faces a shocking revelation that upends his baseball career. A police investigation uncovers that 'Devin Bennett’ is actually Barry Kiloman, a 28-year-old man who had been masquerading as his younger cousin to dominate high school baseball. Despite the deceit, Barry remains eligible to play college baseball due to NCAA eligibility rules. This startling discovery sends ripples through the baseball community, with many stunned by the lengths Barry went to relive his athletic glory.

5. Jr. Bowie Help - 3B / OF → Oda, breaks homerun record in California JUCO, beating Mike Moustakas's old record of 24 by 6 to hit 30

Bowie Help, a 3-star junior JUCO player known for his prowess at third base and outfield, makes a seismic impact in the baseball world by shattering Mike Moustakas' California Junior College home run record. With a staggering total of 30 home runs, Bowie eclipses the previous record by six, showcasing his extraordinary power and skill at the plate. His towering and imposing physique earns him the nickname "Frankenstein" among fans and players, a reference to his 'gruesome', oversized body and intimidating presence when batting.
